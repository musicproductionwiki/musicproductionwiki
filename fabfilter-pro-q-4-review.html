What Is the FabFilter Pro-Q 4?

The FabFilter Pro-Q series has been the reference parametric EQ plugin since the Pro-Q 2 was released. It replaced Waves SSL E-Channel as the default first EQ for mixing engineers, and it replaced iZotope's dynamic EQ tools for many mastering engineers who favored its precise, low-latency workflow over iZotope's more analysis-heavy approach.

The Pro-Q 3 held that position for years. The Pro-Q 4 is not a complete redesign — it's a meaningful evolution that addresses the main limitation of the Pro-Q 3: bands were static unless you specifically engaged the dynamic EQ mode. The Pro-Q 4 introduces Spectral Dynamics, which allows any band to respond dynamically based on the signal level at that frequency. This collapses what previously required two separate plugins (a static EQ plus a dynamic EQ) into a single, coherent workflow.

Spectral Dynamics — The Headline Feature

Spectral Dynamics is the most significant addition to the Pro-Q series since linear phase mode was introduced. Here's exactly what it does and why it matters in practice.

In a standard static EQ, a boost or cut is always applied at the set gain regardless of the input level. If you boost 3 kHz by 3 dB to add presence to a vocal, that boost is always on — whether the vocal is quiet and present or loud and already piercing. This is fine for corrective cuts and character boosts, but problematic when a frequency is only an issue during loud passages.

Spectral Dynamics allows any EQ band to instead engage conditionally — boosting or cutting only when the signal at that band's frequency exceeds a threshold. Each band with Spectral Dynamics enabled has its own threshold, ratio, attack, and release. This means you can set a 3 kHz bell to cut only when that frequency gets harsh during loud phrases, leaving the quieter sections with full, natural presence. Or you can set a low-shelf to boost the bass when the track level drops below a threshold — automatically compensating for energy lost during quiet sections.

How to Use Spectral Dynamics

To enable Spectral Dynamics on a band, click the band's node and look for the Spectral Dynamics option in the band controls. Enable it and a new set of parameters appears: Threshold (the level at which the dynamic behavior begins), Range (the maximum gain reduction or boost the dynamics can apply), Attack (how fast the dynamics engage), and Release (how fast they disengage).

The most common use case in mixing: place a high-shelf or upper-mid bell on a harsh instrument (guitar, synth, or overcompressed vocal) and set it to cut dynamically. When the instrument is playing softly, the cut doesn't engage. When loud peaks hit and the high end becomes harsh, the dynamic cut engages automatically. The result feels like the instrument tamed itself — it's less obviously processed than a static cut and more musical than a separate dynamic EQ with a different interface and workflow.

In mastering, Spectral Dynamics is excellent for controlling sibilance and high-frequency energy that only becomes problematic on loud sections of the master. A 6–8 kHz bell set to cut dynamically only when the master hits full level can prevent digitally harsh highs on the loudest parts of a track without touching the quieter, more open sections of the arrangement.

Spectral Dynamics vs. Traditional Dynamic EQ

The key advantage of Spectral Dynamics over a separate dynamic EQ plugin is integration. Everything — static bands, dynamic bands, spectrum analyzer, EQ match, linear phase mode — is in one window with one consistent interface. There's no plugin switching, no A/B between windows to compare curves, no workflow interruption. For engineers who previously used a static EQ plus iZotope Neutron's dynamic EQ or TDR Nova, Spectral Dynamics consolidates that into a single plugin that feels completely natural to use.

The limitation is that Spectral Dynamics is tied to the Pro-Q 4's band architecture — you can't do multiband compression-style processing (where the dry and wet signals are blended) as easily as in a dedicated dynamic EQ. For straightforward dynamic EQ tasks on individual mix elements, Pro-Q 4 Spectral Dynamics replaces the need for a separate tool entirely. For more complex multiband dynamics on a bus, a dedicated dynamic EQ or multiband compressor is still preferable.

Spectrum Grab — Faster Problem Solving

Spectrum Grab is a smaller but immediately practical addition. In the Pro-Q 3, adding a band required either double-clicking in the EQ display to place a node or adjusting frequency manually by dragging nodes to the target frequency. For corrective work where you're hunting for a resonance or a harshness peak, this meant clicking somewhere approximate and then adjusting until it hit the target.

With Spectrum Grab, you can click directly on the spectrum analyzer display — the live frequency visualization of your input signal — to place a band at exactly that frequency. When you see a resonance peak at 2.3 kHz in the analyzer, you click on that peak and a band appears at 2.3 kHz. No frequency guessing, no dragging. For corrective mixing on vocals, acoustic guitars, and room mics, this speeds up the problem-solving workflow significantly.

Other Improvements Over Pro-Q 3

Band Interaction Curves

FabFilter has refined the interaction between adjacent bands in the Pro-Q 4. In the Pro-Q 3, overlapping bands with similar frequencies could sometimes create unexpected combined shapes in the EQ curve. The Pro-Q 4's improved band calculation produces more predictable, musically natural results when multiple bells are active in the same frequency region. For complex mid-range sculpting with several narrow cuts, this results in a cleaner, more controlled overall curve.

Updated Interface

The Pro-Q 4's interface is largely familiar to Pro-Q 3 users — the core layout is unchanged. Refinements include improved contrast in the spectrum analyzer display (easier to read in bright environments), cleaner tooltips, and slightly reorganized band controls that make the Spectral Dynamics parameters feel like a natural extension of the existing interface rather than an add-on. The EQ display itself is now slightly larger on the same screen real estate, which matters at 1080p resolutions.

EQ Match Improvements

EQ Match — which automatically generates an EQ curve to match a reference spectrum — has been improved in the Pro-Q 4. The algorithm now produces more natural-sounding results with fewer bands, and the resulting match curves are more editable — you can smooth the match result or restrict it to specific frequency ranges before applying it. For mastering engineers using EQ match as a starting point for tonal correction, this makes the feature considerably more practical than the Pro-Q 3's implementation.

Zero Latency vs. Natural Phase vs. Linear Phase

Pro-Q 4 offers three phase modes that serve different use cases. Zero Latency is the default: minimum phase processing with no added delay. This is appropriate for all mixing tasks and real-time monitoring. Natural Phase is a proprietary FabFilter mode that reduces phase coloration compared to minimum phase while introducing only a small and constant latency — a useful middle ground for bus processing where transparency is important but Linear Phase's higher CPU cost isn't warranted. Linear Phase applies the EQ curve with zero phase shift at any frequency — critical for mastering and any task where phase integrity between bands is essential. It introduces latency proportional to the LP quality setting.

Exercises

Beginner — Use Spectrum Grab for Corrective EQ

Insert Pro-Q 4 on a vocal or guitar track. Enable the spectrum analyzer and watch the display while the track plays. Look for peaks — frequencies that consistently rise above the surrounding spectrum. When you see one, click directly on it using Spectrum Grab to place a notch or bell band at exactly that frequency. Pull it down by 2–4 dB, listen, and assess whether the problematic frequency is tamed. Compare with the bypass. Practice this on five different tracks in a session — it will train both your eyes to read spectrum analyzers and your ears to identify the tonal problems that correspond to what you see.

Intermediate — Spectral Dynamics for Vocal Harshness

On a lead vocal, use Pro-Q 4 to add a bell band at around 3–5 kHz (wherever the harshness lives for this vocalist — use the spectrum analyzer to find it). Set the band gain to -4 dB as a static cut. Enable Spectral Dynamics on that band. Set the threshold so that the dynamic behavior engages only on the loudest, most piercing phrases (watch the GR indicator on the band). Set ratio to 3:1, attack to 10ms, release to 80ms. Now the cut only engages when the vocal is harsh. Compare it against a purely static cut — the Spectral Dynamics version should sound more natural and preserve more of the vocal's presence during quiet, well-controlled phrases.

Advanced — Spectral Dynamics on a Mix Bus

Insert Pro-Q 4 at the start of your mix bus chain. Add three bands with Spectral Dynamics enabled: a low-shelf at 80 Hz (gentle boost, dynamic — only boosts when the low end drops on sparse sections), a bell at 3–4 kHz (gentle cut, dynamic — only cuts when density of the mix causes mid-range harshness), and a high-shelf at 12 kHz (very gentle boost, static, always on for air). Play through the loudest and quietest sections of your mix and watch how each band engages and disengages in response to the program material. The goal is a mix bus that automatically compensates for level-dependent tonal shifts — bass warmth that increases on sparse verses, harshness control that activates on dense choruses. This is advanced dynamic tonal shaping that previously required multiple plugins.

FAQ

Is FabFilter Pro-Q 4 worth the upgrade from Pro-Q 3?

Yes, especially if you regularly mix dense tracks or deal with masking issues. Spectral Dynamics alone justifies the upgrade for mixing engineers who previously needed a separate dynamic EQ plugin. The interface improvements also make the Pro-Q 4 noticeably faster to use in practice.

What is Spectral Dynamics in FabFilter Pro-Q 4?

Spectral Dynamics is a feature that allows each EQ band in the Pro-Q 4 to behave dynamically — boosting or cutting based on the level of the signal at that frequency, rather than applying a static correction. It combines traditional EQ and dynamic EQ functionality into a single band.

What is the difference between Pro-Q 3 and Pro-Q 4?

The Pro-Q 4 adds Spectral Dynamics (dynamic behavior on any band), Spectrum Grab (click the spectrum analyzer to add bands at the exact frequency you see), improved band interaction curves, and a refined interface. Pro-Q 3 owners get a discounted upgrade price.

Can I use Pro-Q 4 for mastering?

Yes. Pro-Q 4's Linear Phase mode eliminates phase coloration, making it appropriate for mastering where phase integrity matters most. The Spectral Dynamics feature is also useful on masters for gentle frequency-specific limiting of rogue peaks.

Does Pro-Q 4 work on Mac Apple Silicon?

Yes. FabFilter Pro-Q 4 runs natively on Apple Silicon as a universal binary with VST3, AU, and AAX formats. It is fully compatible with macOS Ventura, Sonoma, and Sequoia.

What makes FabFilter Pro-Q the best EQ plugin?

The combination of zero-latency and linear phase modes, up to 24 bands, excellent spectrum analyzer with real-time output comparison, highly musical default filter curves, and an interface that makes complex EQ tasks fast and intuitive. The Pro-Q series has been the industry reference EQ since the Pro-Q 2.

How do I use Spectrum Grab in Pro-Q 4?

Spectrum Grab allows you to click directly on the spectrum analyzer display to place a new EQ band at that frequency. Rather than estimating the frequency by ear, you click on the peak or dip you see in the analyzer and a band is added at exactly that point.

Is FabFilter Pro-Q 4 CPU intensive?

In Zero Latency mode, Pro-Q 4 is extremely efficient. Linear Phase mode increases CPU usage proportionally to the number of active bands and the LP processing order. On modern systems, even multiple instances in Linear Phase mode should not cause significant performance issues.

What is the price of FabFilter Pro-Q 4?

The FabFilter Pro-Q 4 is priced at approximately €179 (single license). Upgrade pricing for existing Pro-Q 3 owners is available at a significant discount. Check fabfilter.com for current pricing and regional availability.

Can Pro-Q 4 match the EQ curve of other plugins?

Yes. Pro-Q 4 includes an EQ match feature that can analyze a reference track and automatically generate an EQ curve to match its spectrum. This is useful for matching the tonal balance of a reference mix or for corrective mastering.

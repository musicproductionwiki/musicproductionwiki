Glossary

Plain-English definitions for every term in music production. From A/D conversion to zero-latency monitoring — if you've seen it in a DAW or tutorial, it's here.

310 articles · Updated regularly · All skill levels
Filter:
🔤
A/D Conversion
The process of converting analog audio signals into digital data. Quality of A/D conversion directly affects recording fidelity.
Signal Chain 2 min read Beginner
→
🔤
Attack (Compression)
The time it takes a compressor to begin reducing gain after the signal exceeds the threshold. Measured in milliseconds.
Dynamics 2 min read Beginner
→
🔤
Bit Depth
The number of bits used to represent each sample in digital audio. Higher bit depth = greater dynamic range. 24-bit is the modern standard.
Digital Audio 3 min read Beginner
→
🔤
Clip / Clipping
Distortion that occurs when a signal exceeds the maximum level of the system. In digital audio, this creates harsh, unpleasant digital distortion.
Levels 2 min read Beginner
→
🔤
Dry/Wet (Mix Knob)
The ratio of unprocessed (dry) signal to processed (wet) signal in an effect plugin. 0% is fully dry; 100% is fully wet.
Effects 2 min read Beginner
→
🔤
Headroom
The space between the average signal level and the maximum level (0 dBFS). Maintaining headroom prevents clipping and allows dynamics.
Levels 2 min read Beginner
→
🔤
LUFS (Loudness Units relative to Full Scale)
A standardized measurement of perceived loudness used for streaming normalization. Most platforms target between −14 and −9 LUFS.
Mastering 3 min read Intermediate
→
🔤
Saturation
Harmonic distortion that adds warmth and character to audio, mimicking the behavior of analog tape, tubes, and transistors.
Effects 3 min read Intermediate

Affiliate Disclosure

Last updated: July 20, 2026

Some links on MusicProductionWiki.com are affiliate links. If you click one and buy something, we may earn a commission — at no additional cost to you. It never changes the price you pay, and it never changes what we recommend.

MusicProductionWiki.com ("MPW", "we", "us") is a reader-supported reference for music producers. To keep the site free, we participate in affiliate programs. This page explains what that means and how we keep it honest, in line with the U.S. Federal Trade Commission's Endorsement Guides (16 CFR Part 255).

1. How affiliate links work

When we link to a product — a plugin, an audio interface, a pair of monitors — that link is sometimes tagged so the retailer knows the visit came from MPW. If you go on to buy, the retailer pays us a small percentage of the sale. The commission comes out of the retailer's margin, not your pocket. You pay exactly what you would have paid otherwise.

2. Programs we participate in

MPW participates in affiliate programs run by retailers and software vendors across the music-production industry. These relationships may change over time as products and partners come and go.

3. Our recommendations are not for sale

This is the part that matters most. A commission never buys a better review, a higher ranking, or a spot on a "best of" list. Our comparisons are scored on the same criteria regardless of whether a product has an affiliate program at all — and plenty of the tools we recommend don't pay us a cent. Where we have a commercial interest, we'd rather you know about it than trust us blindly.

4. How to spot an affiliate link

Affiliate links point to retailers and product pages (for example, a "Check price" or "Buy" link). Internal links to other MPW articles, tools, and Bible entries are never affiliate links. If you'd prefer not to use ours, you can always search for the product directly — it makes no difference to what you pay.

5. Analytics and cookies

Affiliate partners may set their own cookies to attribute a referral. That's separate from how MPW itself handles data, which is covered in our Privacy Policy.

6. Questions

If anything here is unclear, or you spot a link that isn't disclosed properly, tell us — we take it seriously. Email team@musicproductionwiki.com.

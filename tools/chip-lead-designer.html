Copy the recipe into your synth or DAW
SourceA pulse/square oscillator with an adjustable duty cycle (12.5 / 25 / 50%). Use two pulse voices: one for lead, one for harmony.
ChordsOne voice can’t hold a chord — cycle the triad fast (1/16–1/64, near audio rate) so the ear fuses it. Automate the note, not a held chord.
BassA triangle wave an octave or two down. On real hardware it has no volume control — just on/off — so keep it steady.
PercussionWhite noise through a fast decay envelope: short for hats, longer with a pitch drop for kicks and snares.
MovementDuty-cycle changes plus a touch of vibrato give the static waveform life without any effects.
TempoFree — town/menu themes ~80–110, stage music 120–140, boss themes 150–180 BPM.

Free route: any DAW’s stock pulse/square synth + a triangle for bass. Purpose-built chip plug-ins add authentic quirks, but stock instruments are plenty.

Chiptune lead FAQ

What makes a chiptune lead sound 8-bit?

A pulse (square) wave with an adjustable duty cycle, played under a strict voice budget. The pulse channel’s only tone control is that duty cycle, and cycling a chord’s notes on one voice very fast fakes harmony. Drag the pad here: right for a thinner, more nasal timbre, up until the arpeggio fuses into a chord.

Why only three timbres from four duty cycles?

The 2A03 offers 12.5%, 25%, 50% and 75%, but a 25% and a 75% pulse are mirror images that sound identical. That leaves three genuinely distinct timbres per channel: thin/nasal (12.5%), reedy (25%) and hollow square (50%).

How do you play a chord with one pulse channel?

You cycle the chord’s notes on that one channel extremely fast — a 1/16 to 1/64 arpeggio, often changing every frame — so the ear fuses them into one perceived chord. Raising the arp rate above shows the notes stop sounding separate.

Is the audio a sample?

No — it’s a Web Audio synth generating the pulse lead live in your browser, band-limited so it never aliases. Nothing is recorded, downloaded or uploaded.

What Your Mix Fingerprint Actually Tells You

Every mix has a sonic identity — a distribution of energy across frequency, width, dynamics, and presence that defines how it feels to listen to. The Mix Fingerprint makes that identity visible. Six axes, each measured or rated on a 0–10 scale, plotted against the genre benchmark your peers are working toward and the reference tracks that defined the sound you're chasing.

The value isn't in the numbers. It's in the shape. A mix with a wide, lopsided fingerprint — heavy bass, thin mids, narrow stereo image — looks like a specific kind of problem before you even listen. The gap between your shape and the benchmark ghost is where the work is.

Bass Weight — energy concentration in the 20–200Hz range relative to total spectral energy. High in trap and EDM. Low in folk and classical.

High End Air — energy in the 10kHz–20kHz band. What makes a mix feel open, expensive, and modern.

Stereo Width — mid/side energy ratio. Ambient music lives at the far right of this axis. Drill lives toward the left.

Dynamic Range — crest factor: the difference between peaks and average loudness. High means breathing room. Low means the limiter has been working too hard.

Vocal Presence — how upfront the lead vocal sits. We can't measure this from a full mix accurately — only you know what you intended.

Midrange Clarity — how clean the 2–5kHz presence region is. Where vocals cut, guitars bite, and snares crack. Where mud lives.

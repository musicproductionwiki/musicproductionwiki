iZotope Ozone 11 Review: Is It the Best Mastering Plugin?

MP
The Music Production Wiki Team
Updated May 2025 12 min read
Quick Verdict

iZotope Ozone 11 is the most complete mastering plugin suite available. Master Assistant remains the best AI-assisted mastering starting point on the market. The new Stem Focus module — separating and independently processing stems within a stereo mix — is a genuinely novel capability. At $499 for Standard or $999 for Advanced at full price, it's expensive, but frequent sales and bundle deals make it significantly more accessible. If you master music professionally or seriously as an independent artist, Ozone is the benchmark.

iZotope Ozone has been the mastering plugin standard for over a decade. Ozone 11, released in 2023, builds on that foundation with meaningful new tools rather than cosmetic updates. This review covers everything new in Ozone 11, the core mastering chain, how Master Assistant has improved, and honest assessment of who needs it and who doesn't.

What we'll cover: What's new in Ozone 11, the full module roster, Master Assistant workflow, Stem Focus explained, Stabilizer, the Limiter improvements, pricing tiers, integration with Neutron, and verdict by user type.

What's New in Ozone 11

Stem Focus: The flagship new feature. Stem Focus uses AI to separate a stereo mix into stem components — vocals, drums, bass, other instruments — and allows independent processing of each stem within Ozone's mastering chain. This gives mastering engineers a level of surgical precision previously unavailable in the mastering stage: if the bass is slightly loud in the mix, Stem Focus can reduce just the bass component without affecting the rest. If the vocals need more presence, Stem Focus can target just the vocal component. This doesn't replace a proper mix revision, but it provides meaningful correction capability without going back to the session.

Stabilizer: A new dynamics module that identifies and controls resonant frequencies in the master — specific frequency ranges that jump out dynamically because of resonances in the mix or the mix room. Stabilizer acts like a dynamic EQ targeting resonances specifically, reducing frequency imbalances that are hard to catch with static EQ. Particularly useful for mixes from untreated rooms where specific low-mid frequencies are artificially boosted by room modes.

Improved Master Assistant: Master Assistant's AI analysis has been updated with a larger training dataset and improved genre recognition. The starting points it generates are noticeably more appropriate to genre context — EDM masters are handled differently from classical masters, trap production differently from folk. The improved Loudness Matching references more accurately reflect current streaming platform targets.

Updated Limiter: Ozone 11's Limiter module includes a new IRC V mode (Intelligent Release Control) designed to handle modern loudness targets with fewer artifacts at high gain levels. The improved limiting algorithm preserves more transient information at -9 to -11 LUFS targets, reducing the pumping and breathing artifacts that older limiting approaches introduced at these loudness levels.

The Full Module Roster

Ozone 11 Standard includes: EQ, Dynamic EQ, Vintage EQ, Maximizer (limiter), Dynamics (compressor/expander), Vintage Compressor, Imager (stereo width), Low End Focus, Stabilizer, Stem Focus, and Master Assistant. Ozone 11 Advanced adds: Vintage Tape, Vintage Limiter, and full access to Codec Preview (previewing how the master will sound after codec compression on streaming platforms).

The module quality is consistently high. The EQ is clean and precise for surgical moves or gentle tonal shaping. The Dynamic EQ handles program-dependent equalization — boosting or cutting based on the incoming signal level — which is particularly useful on the master bus where static boosts can cause problems on certain passages while helping others. The Imager provides M-S (mid-side) control over stereo width without phase issues. The Vintage modules add analogue warmth and character for masters that need more than clinical transparency.

Master Assistant Workflow

Master Assistant is where most producers start with Ozone, and understanding how to use it effectively separates good results from great ones. The workflow: import a reference track (a commercially released song in your genre that represents the sound you're targeting), trigger Master Assistant analysis on your mix, and let the AI generate a complete Ozone processing chain. Master Assistant analyses both your mix and the reference track, then configures modules to move your mix closer to the reference's tonal balance, dynamics, and loudness.

The generated chain is a starting point, not a finished result. Engage every module in the chain, listen carefully, and begin adjusting. The AI's loudness target is usually accurate. Its EQ suggestions need evaluation against your specific mix — the reference is a genre guide, not an exact target. The dynamics settings may be too aggressive or too gentle depending on how close your mix already is to the reference.

Effective Master Assistant use: always use a reference track rather than letting the AI work without one. Choose references that are recent (within the last 3–5 years) and from artists your listeners would recognise as successful in your genre. Use multiple references if your track spans genre boundaries.

Stem Focus in Practice

Stem Focus is more practically useful than initial skepticism about AI stem separation in a mastering context suggests. The use cases where it genuinely helps: mix arrived with the kick slightly too heavy (Stem Focus reduces the drum component by 1–1.5 dB, solving the problem without going back to the mix session), vocals slightly recessed (Stem Focus boosts the vocal component by 0.5–1 dB, adding presence while keeping the rest of the master balanced), or bass needs more mid-range presence for playback on laptop speakers (Stem Focus adds saturation specifically to the bass component).

The separation quality is comparable to other AI stem separation tools — good on modern pop and electronic material, less reliable on dense acoustic arrangements. The processing within each stem is the more interesting capability: you're not just adjusting levels of separated stems but applying EQ, compression, and saturation specifically to each component.

Pricing

VersionFull PriceKey Additions
Ozone Elements$49Basic Master Assistant, limited modules
Ozone Standard$499Full module set, Stem Focus, Stabilizer
Ozone Advanced$999Vintage modules, Codec Preview, full suite
Music Production Suite$999Ozone + Neutron + RX + other iZotope tools

Full list price is rarely what anyone pays. iZotope runs sales of 40–60% off several times per year, making Ozone Standard accessible at $200–300. The Music Production Suite, which includes Ozone, Neutron, and RX at the Advanced tier, is the best value entry into the full iZotope ecosystem if you're buying all three tools anyway. Upgrade pricing from previous Ozone versions is significantly reduced.

vs. Key Alternatives

vs. FabFilter Pro-L2 + Pro-Q3 ($149 + $179): FabFilter's limiter and EQ are individually excellent and their combination is widely used for mastering. FabFilter tools don't have Master Assistant or Stem Focus, but their per-module quality is arguably higher than Ozone's equivalent modules. For producers who prefer building a mastering chain module by module with full manual control, FabFilter is the alternative. Ozone wins on integrated workflow and AI assistance.

vs. LANDR ($9–39/month): LANDR is cloud-based, no offline access, requires subscription, and produces a finished master without intermediate control. Ozone is a full plugin with complete manual control and local processing. LANDR is faster for batch mastering or casual releases. Ozone is appropriate when the master quality matters enough to warrant professional-level attention.

Verdict

Ozone 11 earns its position as the mastering plugin benchmark. Stem Focus and Stabilizer are genuine workflow additions rather than marketing features. Master Assistant has improved meaningfully. The module quality across the suite is consistently professional. For independent artists who master their own music, Ozone Standard at sale price is the clearest upgrade recommendation available. For professional mastering engineers, Ozone Advanced's Codec Preview and Vintage modules justify the higher tier. For casual producers releasing infrequently, the Elements tier or LANDR may be sufficient.

Score: 9.0/10

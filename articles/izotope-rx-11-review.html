iZotope RX 11 Review: Still the Best Audio Repair Suite?

By MusicProductionWiki Staff • Updated May 2026 • 15 min read

⚡ Quick Verdict

Score: 9/10. iZotope RX 11 remains the most comprehensive audio repair suite available in 2026. The Standard tier ($399) is the recommended entry point for most producers and podcasters — it includes Dialogue Isolate with de-reverb, Music Rebalance for stem separation, Streaming Preview, Loudness Optimize, and Spectral Repair. New machine learning improvements make the Repair Assistant faster and more accurate than previous versions. The Advanced tier ($1,199) is genuinely worth it for professional post-production work. Worth upgrading from RX 10? Yes for Standard/Advanced users. RX Elements users should jump to Standard.

iZotope RX has been the audio repair tool of choice for professional engineers since RX 2, earning two Engineering Emmy Awards and a Scientific and Engineering Award from the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences. The premise is simple: take difficult, problematic audio and make it usable. What RX actually does — given enough time and skill — approaches the miraculous: dialogue recorded in moving vehicles becomes broadcast-quality; interviews ruined by HVAC noise become clean; music with clipped peaks has those peaks reconstructed.

RX 11, released in May 2024, continues this tradition with meaningful upgrades focused on AI-powered dialogue processing, streaming-specific features for the modern delivery landscape, and expanded DAW integration through ARA 2 support. This review covers what's new, what's genuinely useful, and how to decide between the three pricing tiers.

What's New in RX 11: Key Features Assessed

Dialogue Isolate — The Standout Upgrade

Dialogue Isolate is RX's AI-powered voice isolation module that separates speech from background noise, ambient sound, and room reverb. In RX 11, it received three significant improvements that make it genuinely competitive with dedicated voice isolation tools:

Built-in de-reverb: RX 11's Dialogue Isolate now includes reverb reduction processing alongside noise isolation. In previous versions, achieving clean vocal isolation from a reverberant room required combining Dialogue Isolate with a separate De-reverb module. RX 11 integrates the two, producing cleaner results in one pass. The Advanced tier adds multi-band de-reverb processing for more targeted treatment of reverb across different frequency ranges.

Available in Standard tier: Dialogue Isolate was previously an Advanced-only feature. RX 11 Standard now includes the core Dialogue Isolate module — a significant expansion of what the middle tier provides, and the primary reason to upgrade from RX 10 Standard to RX 11 Standard.

VST/AU/AAX plugin: Dialogue Isolate can now be used as a real-time plugin within your DAW rather than only as a module inside the RX application. This changes the workflow substantially — you can apply Dialogue Isolate during mixing without exporting to the RX standalone application and back.

Performance: in testing across multiple scenarios — remote interviews with room reverb, dialogue recorded in untreated home offices, voice-over with HVAC noise — Dialogue Isolate in RX 11 performs at or above the level of dedicated competitors like Waves Clarity Vx and Supertone Clear for most use cases. For particularly challenging scenarios (extreme reverb, metallic room acoustics), competing tools occasionally outperform it on specific tasks. The advantage of RX's Dialogue Isolate is its integration with the full suite — you can combine it with De-noise, Spectral Repair, and other modules for comprehensive cleanup that standalone voice isolation plugins can't match.

Streaming Preview — The Most Practically Useful New Feature

Streaming Preview is a new RX 11 feature that lets you audition how your audio will sound after encoding by major streaming platforms. When you release music to Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube, or Tidal, the platform transcodes your original audio file to their distribution format — typically OGG Vorbis at 320kbps (Spotify), AAC at 256kbps (Apple Music), or AAC/Opus at variable bitrates (YouTube). These encoding algorithms introduce subtle but sometimes significant artifacts: slight changes in high-frequency detail, stereo imaging shifts, and transient softening.

Streaming Preview simulates these encoding artifacts so you can hear what your music will sound like on each platform before submission. The practical use: if your mix has delicate high-frequency content that encoding artifacts could harm (detailed acoustic guitar, complex cymbals, synthesizer textures), you can identify this before release and make compensating mix adjustments.

This feature is genuinely useful for mastering engineers and producers who care about exactly how their work sounds on each platform. It's less critical for producers whose music is dense and full-frequency (electronic, hip-hop) where encoding artifacts are typically inaudible. But as a first-in-class feature that no competing tool currently matches, it's a significant addition to RX's toolset.

Loudness Optimize — Practical for Mastering Workflow

Loudness Optimize automates the process of matching your audio to platform-specific loudness targets (measured in LUFS — Loudness Units relative to Full Scale). Spotify targets -14 LUFS integrated; Apple Music targets -16 LUFS; YouTube targets -14 LUFS. Without loudness targeting, your release gets normalized by each platform — if your master is louder than the target, the platform turns it down; if quieter, some platforms leave it as-is (quiet tracks suffer on playlist alongside louder ones).

Loudness Optimize analyzes your audio and applies processing to meet your target LUFS while preserving dynamics and preventing clipping. It's not a replacement for proper mastering — a loudness-optimized track still needs careful master EQ, limiting, and stereo field decisions made by an engineer. But as a final delivery step to ensure platform compliance, it's a time-saver that reduces manual calculation and limiter tweaking.

ARA 2 Integration — Improved DAW Workflow

ARA 2 (Audio Random Access) allows the RX Spectral Editor to operate directly inside compatible DAWs without the traditional "send to RX and send back" workflow. In Logic Pro 10.7+, Pro Tools 2024.6+, and Studio One 6.0+, RX 11's Spectral Editor appears as an in-DAW editing environment — you can highlight a section of a region in your timeline, invoke the Spectral Editor, remove a noise or artifact, and return to your session without file exports or imports.

This integration meaningfully reduces friction for detailed spectral editing work. The traditional RX workflow required: render from DAW → open in RX standalone → edit → export → import back to DAW — typically 4–6 steps per edit. ARA 2 reduces this to approximately 2 steps (invoke, edit, close). For producers doing light cleanup work (removing a noise in one section, fixing a click, spectral repair of a brief artifact), the ARA workflow is substantially more practical.

Note: ARA 2 integration currently works in Rosetta emulation mode on Apple Silicon Macs for Logic Pro — native M-series chip support is in development. Intel Mac and Windows users get full ARA 2 performance.

Music Rebalance — Improved Stem Separation

Music Rebalance allows you to isolate or suppress specific elements within a mixed track — vocals, bass, drums, and other instruments — using AI stem separation. RX 11 brings an improved algorithm that produces better isolation quality with fewer artifacts, particularly for bass and drum separation where previous versions occasionally blurred boundaries with adjacent frequency content.

Music Rebalance serves several workflows: extracting vocal stems for remix licensing, reducing bass or drum levels in a mix you don't have stems for, creating instrumental versions from completed mixes, and sample extraction for producers who want to sample specific elements of recorded tracks. The quality is competitive with dedicated stem separation tools like Moises and Lalal.ai for most use cases, with the advantage of being integrated into the full RX repair workflow.

Repair Assistant: Machine Learning Gets Better

Repair Assistant is RX's smart analysis tool — it listens to your audio, identifies problems, and suggests (or automatically applies) repair modules with appropriate settings. RX 11's Repair Assistant uses upgraded machine learning algorithms that produce better initial suggestions and require less manual adjustment to achieve good results.

The practical workflow: import problematic audio, run Repair Assistant, accept or adjust the suggestions, apply. For producers and podcasters who don't have time to master the full RX toolset, Repair Assistant dramatically lowers the learning curve. The results are not always perfect — extreme or unusual audio problems still benefit from manual module work — but the Assistant handles 80–90% of common cleanup tasks adequately in a single pass.

The honest competitive assessment: for any single task — voice noise reduction, stem separation, dialogue de-reverb — there are cheaper specialized tools that perform comparably or better. Waves Clarity Vx at $100 is excellent for simple voice noise reduction. Supertone Clear and dxRevive compete credibly with RX on dialogue isolation benchmarks.

Where RX 11 wins is breadth and integration. No other tool gives you spectral editing + stem separation + noise reduction + loudness tools + streaming preview + DAW integration in a single application. For producers who need all of these — or who regularly encounter diverse audio problems requiring different tools — the comprehensive RX package justifies the $399 Standard investment over accumulating multiple single-purpose alternatives.

Who Should Buy RX 11

Music producers: RX 11 Standard is one of the most genuinely useful tools in a producer's plugin arsenal. The applications in music production are extensive: removing noise from poorly recorded samples, cleaning up vocal recordings made in untreated spaces, repairing clicks and pops in archived recordings, separating stems from reference tracks for study, and preparing masters for streaming platform delivery.

Podcasters and content creators: Dialogue Isolate and De-noise together handle the most common audio cleanup challenges in podcast production — room reverb and background noise. Combined with Repair Assistant for automatic problem detection, RX Standard provides professional podcast audio cleanup capability at a price most serious podcasters can justify.

Post-production professionals: RX Advanced remains the industry standard for film, television, and broadcast audio repair. The Dialogue Contour module (Advanced only) allows AI-driven adjustment of vocal inflection — a capability with few equivalents. The 44-module breadth covers effectively every audio repair scenario in professional post-production work.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is iZotope RX 11 worth buying?

Yes for anyone who regularly needs audio cleanup. RX 11 Standard ($399) offers Dialogue Isolate with de-reverb, Music Rebalance, Streaming Preview, Loudness Optimize, and 33 modules. It's the most comprehensive audio repair suite available and has earned Engineering Emmy Awards and Academy recognition. The Repair Assistant makes it accessible even without deep expertise.

What is new in iZotope RX 11?

Major additions: upgraded Dialogue Isolate with built-in de-reverb and VST/AU/AAX plugin availability; Streaming Preview to audition platform encoding artifacts before release; Loudness Optimize for LUFS targeting; ARA 2 integration in Logic Pro, Pro Tools, and Studio One; improved Music Rebalance algorithm; upgraded Repair Assistant machine learning; Mid/Side display mode.

What is the difference between RX 11 Elements, Standard, and Advanced?

Elements ($99): 9 modules, basic noise removal. Standard ($399): 33 modules, adds Dialogue Isolate, Music Rebalance, Spectral Repair, Streaming Preview, Loudness Optimize. Advanced ($1,199): 44 modules, adds Dialogue Contour, DeBleed, advanced multi-band processing. Standard is the right choice for most music producers and podcasters.

Can iZotope RX 11 remove background noise?

Yes — multiple tools: De-noise (broadband), De-hum (electrical hum), De-click, De-crackle, De-clip, and Dialogue Isolate (AI voice isolation that separates speech from all background noise including reverb). Repair Assistant automatically suggests the right combination based on analysis of your specific audio.

Does iZotope RX 11 work as a plugin?

Yes — three modes: standalone application, VST/AU/AAX plugin via RX Connect workflow, and ARA 2 integration for in-DAW spectral editing in Logic Pro 10.7+, Pro Tools 2024.6+, and Studio One 6.0+. Dialogue Isolate now works as a standalone plugin without requiring the full RX application.

How does iZotope RX 11 compare to Waves Clarity Vx?

Clarity Vx (~$100) excels at real-time voice noise reduction — simple, fast, focused. RX 11 Standard ($399) is a comprehensive suite with spectral editing, stem separation, streaming tools, and 33 modules covering every audio repair scenario. For voice noise reduction only: Clarity Vx is excellent value. For comprehensive audio repair across diverse problems: RX 11 is unmatched in breadth.

What is iZotope RX Dialogue Isolate?

AI-powered voice isolation that separates speech from background noise and reverb using machine learning. RX 11 adds built-in de-reverb to Dialogue Isolate, makes it available in Standard tier (previously Advanced-only), and enables it as a standalone VST/AU/AAX plugin for real-time DAW use.

What is iZotope RX Streaming Preview?

A new RX 11 feature that simulates how your audio will sound after streaming platform encoding (Spotify OGG, Apple Music AAC, YouTube Opus). Platform transcoding introduces subtle artifacts — Streaming Preview lets you hear these before release and make compensating mix adjustments. First-in-class feature with no direct equivalent in competing tools.

Frequently Asked Questions

+ FAQ What are the key differences between iZotope RX 11 Standard and Advanced tiers?

Standard ($399) includes 33 modules with core tools like Dialogue Isolate, Music Rebalance, and Spectral Repair, while Advanced ($1,199) adds 11 extra modules including Dialogue Contour, DeBleed, advanced multi-band Dialogue Isolate, Voice De-noise, and expanded batch processing. Advanced is recommended for professional post-production and film/TV work, whereas Standard suits most music producers and podcasters.

+ FAQ Does RX 11 support ARA 2 integration with DAWs?

Yes, RX 11 includes expanded DAW integration through ARA 2 support, allowing for seamless plugin workflow directly within your digital audio workstation. This eliminates the need to export audio to the standalone RX application for many repair tasks.

+ FAQ What is Dialogue Isolate and how does it work in RX 11?

Dialogue Isolate is a machine learning-powered tool that separates dialogue from background noise and uses de-reverb technology to clean up vocals. It's available in all three RX 11 tiers, with the Advanced version offering multi-band control for more precise dialogue processing.

+ FAQ How does Music Rebalance function in RX 11?

Music Rebalance is an AI-powered stem separation tool available in Standard and Advanced tiers that allows you to isolate and adjust individual elements of a mixed track, such as drums, vocals, and instruments. This is particularly useful for remixing, creating instrumental versions, or salvaging problematic recordings.

+ FAQ What is the Streaming Preview feature and why is it useful?

Streaming Preview is a Standard and Advanced tier feature that lets you preview your audio across multiple streaming platform loudness specifications (Spotify, YouTube, Apple Music, etc.) before final delivery. This ensures your repairs and mastering decisions translate correctly across different streaming services.

+ FAQ Should I upgrade from RX 10 to RX 11?

Yes, upgrading is worthwhile if you use Standard or Advanced tiers, thanks to improved machine learning in the Repair Assistant that makes repairs faster and more accurate. RX Elements users should consider jumping to Standard, as the price-to-feature ratio represents significant added value.

+ FAQ What recognition has iZotope RX earned for audio repair?

iZotope RX has earned two Engineering Emmy Awards and a Scientific and Engineering Award from the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences, establishing it as the industry standard for professional audio repair since RX 2.

+ FAQ Which RX 11 tier should a beginner choose?

RX 11 Elements at $99 is ideal for beginners and occasional users needing basic noise removal with 9 essential modules including Repair Assistant, De-noise, De-click, and De-clip. However, most music producers and podcasters should invest in Standard ($399) for significantly better tools like Dialogue Isolate and Music Rebalance.

DistroKid Review 2026: Is It Still the Best Music Distributor?

By MusicProductionWiki Staff • Updated May 2026 • 14 min read

⚡ Quick Verdict

Score: 8.5/10. Best for prolific artists who release regularly. DistroKid's $24.99/year unlimited upload model is the most cost-effective distribution for artists releasing 4+ tracks per year. 100% royalties from DSPs, 2-5 day delivery, and a clean interface make it the default recommendation. The catch: music disappears if you cancel. Leave a Legacy ($29/single, $49/album) is effectively mandatory for any release you want to keep live long-term — factor that into your real cost. Artists releasing one project a year and wanting permanent placement without subscription risk should look at CD Baby first.

DistroKid launched in 2013 with a proposition that felt radical at the time: unlimited uploads, 100% of royalties, flat annual fee. In an era when CD Baby charged per-release and kept 9% of earnings forever, the model was genuinely disruptive. In 2026, DistroKid is the most widely used independent music distributor globally — and the landscape around it has evolved considerably.

Competitors have adopted similar pricing. TuneCore now takes 0% royalties. SoundOn (TikTok's distributor) is free. RouteNote offers a free tier. The question is no longer whether DistroKid's model is unique — it's whether it remains the best overall option for the majority of independent artists. The answer is mostly yes, with important caveats that most DistroKid reviews don't cover honestly.

The Leave a Legacy Issue: Your Music Disappears When You Stop Paying

This is the most critical thing to understand about DistroKid and the feature most underplayed in reviews: if you cancel your DistroKid subscription or fail to renew, your music gets removed from every streaming platform. Spotify. Apple Music. Amazon. All of them. Gone.

This isn't a technical limitation — it's a business model decision. DistroKid's contracts with DSPs are contingent on active subscriptions. The moment your payment lapses, DistroKid removes your releases.

Leave a Legacy ($29 per single, $49 per album) is DistroKid's solution: a one-time payment per release that transfers the permanence — your music stays live even if you cancel. The problem is that this cost is add-on to every release, accumulating across your catalog, and must be purchased at release time (or later through the dashboard). Artists who release 10 singles over two years and then want to take a break discover they owe $290 in Leave a Legacy fees to keep those songs live during their hiatus, or lose the catalog entirely.

For prolific artists who intend to maintain their subscription indefinitely, this is a non-issue. For anyone who might take a break, change distributors, or simply forget to renew a credit card, it's a genuine trap that has caught many artists. Know going in: with DistroKid, catalog permanence is optional and costs extra per release.

Royalties: What "100% of Royalties" Actually Means

DistroKid advertises 100% of royalties, and for basic DSP streaming (Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon Music, Tidal), this is accurate. Every dollar those platforms pay for streams of your music goes to you. DistroKid makes no royalty deduction from DSP payouts.

The exception: Social Media Pack and Content ID. When you use DistroKid's Social Media Pack to monetize your music on TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, and YouTube, DistroKid keeps 20% of that monetization revenue and passes 80% to you. This is stated in DistroKid's terms and is disclosed, but it's not part of the "100% royalties" headline. The 20% cut applies specifically to the social media monetization — not to your Spotify or Apple Music earnings.

For most artists, DSP streaming royalties significantly outweigh social media monetization, so the practical impact of the 20% social cut is modest. For artists whose primary audience engagement is through TikTok or YouTube shorts, it's more material.

Distribution Speed: DistroKid's Genuine Advantage

DistroKid is consistently one of the fastest distributors. Delivery to Spotify and Apple Music typically happens within 2-5 days. Some stores receive music within 24 hours. For artists releasing on a specific date tied to a social media campaign or trending moment, the speed advantage is real.

Comparing delivery speeds: TuneCore's delivery is 1-7 days (similar to DistroKid). CD Baby runs 1-3 weeks for initial release. RouteNote can take 1-4 weeks due to manual review. SoundOn (TikTok) is fast for TikTok but slower for other platforms. DistroKid and TuneCore are the clear speed leaders.

What Musician Plus Adds vs Musician

The Musician plan at $24.99 covers basic unlimited distribution but omits several features that matter for serious release strategy: you cannot schedule a future release date (critical for Spotify pre-save campaigns and playlist pitching), you cannot set a custom label name on your releases, and you only get basic stats rather than daily sales data.

Musician Plus at approximately $35-44.99/year adds all three. For artists working with music blogs, playlist curators, or running any kind of release campaign, the ability to schedule a future release date is essentially required — pitching to Spotify's editorial team requires a track live in their system before the public release date, which requires release scheduling. If this matters to you, Musician Plus is the plan to buy from the start.

Who DistroKid Is Best For

✅ DistroKid Is Right For You If:

  • You release music consistently — 5+ singles or 2+ albums per year
  • Speed matters for your release strategy (social trends, campaigns)
  • You want 100% of DSP royalties and don't mind paying per-feature for add-ons
  • You plan to maintain your subscription long-term and catalog permanence via Leave a Legacy is part of your budget
  • You run collaborative projects and need automated royalty splits
  • You're building a catalog quickly and subscription economics favor you over per-release fees

⚠️ Consider Alternatives If:

  • You release one or two projects a year — CD Baby or TuneCore's per-release model may cost less
  • You want catalog permanence without ongoing fees or Leave a Legacy add-ons
  • You're a hobbyist who may stop paying in a year — your music disappears
  • You need structured customer support with phone or live chat access
  • You want publishing administration bundled with distribution (neither DistroKid nor TuneCore handles publishing; look at DistroKid's Publishing add-on or dedicated publishers like SongTrust)
  • Your primary distribution goal is TikTok — SoundOn (free, direct from TikTok) is worth evaluating

The Add-Ons: Skip or Buy?

Leave a Legacy: Buy it for any release you care about. At $29 per single, it's the cost of catalog permanence. If you're releasing music you intend to generate revenue from for years, buy it at release time. Don't buy it speculatively on demo singles or experimental tracks you may remove anyway.

YouTube Content ID: Evaluate carefully. Content ID monetizes third-party use of your music on YouTube by claiming ad revenue on videos that use your tracks. The often-missed consequence: music channels that want to feature your track will see a Content ID claim and may choose not to use your music at all — the channel loses ad revenue, not you, but they also lose the ability to promote your work without penalty. For artists with mainstream or commercially released music seeking monetization, Content ID makes sense. For emerging artists building audience through playlist coverage and organic sharing, it can actively hurt growth by penalizing channels that would have promoted you.

Shazam: Probably worth it at $0.99/track/year. People who hear your music in public can identify it. Costs almost nothing.

Store Maximizer: Probably skip it. You can add releases to new stores manually at no cost. Store Maximizer automates this for $7.95/album/year — a convenience fee that's rarely worth it unless you have a large catalog and genuinely don't have time to add stores manually.

Support: DistroKid's Weakest Point

DistroKid's customer support is ticket-based — no phone, no live chat. Response times average 24-48 hours in non-peak periods and can stretch longer during busy release seasons. For most routine distribution tasks, this is fine. For time-sensitive issues — a release stuck in processing the day before your campaign launch, a payout discrepancy, a metadata error that needs fixing immediately — the lack of urgent support options is a real limitation.

The self-service help center is comprehensive and covers most common issues, which mitigates this somewhat. But artists who need human intervention for critical problems have found DistroKid's support channels frustrating in scenarios where speed matters.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does DistroKid cost in 2026?

The Musician base plan is $24.99/year for unlimited uploads under one artist name. Musician Plus (adds release scheduling, label name, daily stats) costs approximately $35-44.99/year. Add-ons raise the true cost: Leave a Legacy ($29/single, $49/album one-time), Content ID ($4.95/single/year or $14.95/album/year), Shazam ($0.99/song/year), Store Maximizer ($7.95/album/year).

Does DistroKid keep any of your royalties?

No for DSP streaming (Spotify, Apple Music, etc.) — DistroKid keeps 0%. Yes for social media monetization — DistroKid keeps 20% of UGC monetization revenue from TikTok, Instagram/Facebook, and YouTube when you use their Social Media Pack. Standard payment processor fees apply to payouts.

What happens to your music if you cancel DistroKid?

Your music gets removed from all streaming platforms. This is DistroKid's most significant structural risk. Leave a Legacy ($29/single, $49/album one-time) keeps releases live permanently after cancellation. Without it, your entire catalog disappears when your subscription lapses.

How fast does DistroKid distribute music?

Typically 2-5 days to major platforms including Spotify and Apple Music. Some stores within 24 hours. This makes DistroKid one of the two fastest distributors (alongside TuneCore) — a genuine advantage for time-sensitive releases tied to campaigns or trends.

Is DistroKid better than TuneCore?

For prolific artists (5+ releases/year), DistroKid's flat fee is significantly cheaper. TuneCore charges per release ($9.99/single/year, $29.99/album/year) — that's $50 for five singles versus DistroKid's $24.99 base. For occasional artists releasing one project annually, TuneCore's per-release model can be cheaper. Both keep 0% of DSP royalties.

Is DistroKid better than CD Baby?

For prolific artists, yes — DistroKid's unlimited model beats CD Baby's per-release fees. CD Baby's key advantage: one-time fees that keep releases live permanently without subscription risk. CD Baby also permanently takes 9% of royalties, which compounds significantly as earnings grow. For artists prioritizing catalog permanence, CD Baby's one-time model is worth the higher upfront cost.

What is Leave a Legacy on DistroKid?

A one-time fee per release ($29/single, $49/album) that ensures your release stays on streaming platforms permanently even after canceling your DistroKid subscription. Without it, canceling your subscription removes your music from all stores. For any release intended to generate long-term revenue, Leave a Legacy is effectively mandatory.

Does DistroKid distribute to all major streaming platforms?

Yes — Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon Music, YouTube Music, Tidal, Deezer, Pandora, iHeartRadio, TikTok, Instagram/Facebook, and dozens more globally. TikTok and Instagram/Facebook are included in the base plan. Some regional or newer platforms require the Store Maximizer add-on or manual addition through the dashboard.

Music Streaming Royalties Explained: How You Actually Get Paid

First published May 2026
MP
The Music Production Wiki Team
May 2026
Quick Answer

Streaming platforms do not pay a fixed per-stream rate. They use a "streamshare" model: each platform allocates approximately 70% of its total monthly revenue to a royalty pool, then divides that pool among all rights holders in proportion to their share of total streams for that month. This means the per-stream rate fluctuates monthly based on platform revenue and total global stream count. The approximate result: Spotify pays $0.003-0.005 per stream at the master level, Apple Music pays $0.007-0.01, YouTube pays $0.001-0.003. These figures go to the master rights holder; composition royalties are separate.

The Streamshare Model Explained

Every month, each streaming platform calculates its total revenue -- subscriptions plus advertising. It allocates approximately 70% of this revenue to rights holders as the royalty pool. It then determines what percentage of total platform streams each recording received that month. A recording that received 0.001% of all Spotify streams that month receives 0.001% of the Spotify royalty pool for that month. This pro-rata model means that as Spotify grows its subscriber base, the pool grows -- but as the total number of streams also grows (more artists, more content), each individual stream's share of the pool shrinks. The result: per-stream rates have declined slowly over time as streaming has grown.

What You Actually Receive

From each stream, the platform pays the master rights holder (typically 50-70% of the total stream royalty). From that master royalty, the record label takes their share if one is involved, and the remainder goes to the artist through the distribution agreement. If you are self-distributed (DistroKid, TuneCore, CD Baby), you receive the master royalty directly minus the distributor's fees. Separately, the platform pays the composition rights holder (typically 30-50% of the total stream royalty) -- this goes through the MLC (for mechanical royalties) and your PRO (for performance royalties). You must be registered with the MLC and a PRO to receive these composition royalties.

Why Per-Stream Rates Vary

The per-stream rate you see in your distributor's reports is affected by: which country the stream came from (premium subscribers in wealthy countries generate more revenue per stream than free-tier listeners in developing markets), whether the listener was on a paid or free subscription (paid streams pay more than ad-supported free-tier streams), and which month the streams occurred in (total platform revenue and total stream count fluctuate monthly). A stream from a paying subscriber in the US or UK is worth approximately 5-10x a free-tier stream in a market with lower advertising rates.

Realistic Income Numbers

At Spotify's approximate $0.004 per stream average: 250,000 streams = $1,000 at the master level. 1,000,000 streams = $4,000. 10,000,000 streams = $40,000. These are master royalties only, before distributor fees and before the composition royalties that flow through PRO and MLC channels separately. For an independent artist who writes and records their own music, the total income per stream (master plus composition) is approximately double the master royalty alone -- but only if correctly registered with the MLC and a PRO.

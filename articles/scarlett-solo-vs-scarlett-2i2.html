Focusrite Scarlett Solo vs 2i2: Which Should You Buy?

First published May 2026 · Updated May 2026
MP
The Music Production Wiki Team
Updated May 2026 Beginner → Advanced
Quick Answer

Buy the Scarlett Solo ($119) if you record one thing at a time and are certain that won't change. Buy the Scarlett 2i2 ($169) if there's any chance you'll ever want to record two sources simultaneously — two vocals, guitar and voice at once, two instruments. The $50 difference buys a second preamp that you can't add later. The audio quality of the preamps is identical between the two models.

What's Exactly the Same

The Scarlett Solo and 2i2 share identical preamp circuitry, identical converter quality, identical Air mode implementation, identical software bundle (Hitmaker Expansion), identical USB-C connection, and identical bus-powered operation. The sound quality of recordings made through either interface is indistinguishable. If you record one source at a time, you will never notice a difference between them in your recordings.

The One Meaningful Difference

The 2i2 has two XLR microphone inputs. The Solo has one. That's the entire functional difference. The 2i2 also has a slightly larger chassis to accommodate the second input, but its form factor remains compact. Everything else — the output connections, the headphone output, the direct monitoring, the software — is the same.

When Two Inputs Actually Matter

One microphone input is sufficient for: recording vocals over a beat, recording acoustic guitar as a single track, podcasting solo, recording keyboard through a DI, sampling from hardware gear one channel at a time, voice-over work.

Two microphone inputs become necessary for: recording vocals and acoustic guitar simultaneously (singer-songwriter performance capture), recording a podcast with two hosts in the same room, recording piano with two microphones (one at each end of the keyboard), recording an acoustic duo simultaneously, drum overhead miking (two overhead mics), recording any instrument that benefits from a two-microphone setup (acoustic guitar, piano, room ambience).

The "I'll Upgrade Later" Fallacy

The most common mistake: buying the Solo with the plan to upgrade to the 2i2 later if needed. The problem is that upgrading isn't free — you spend $119 now and $169 later, totaling $288 versus the $169 you'd spend buying the 2i2 immediately. You also spend time setting up and learning a new interface twice. If there's meaningful uncertainty about whether you'll need two inputs, buying the 2i2 now costs $50 and saves a potential $119 later.

⚡ Solo or 2i2?
If you record only one source at a time and that's definitely all you'll ever do → Scarlett Solo. Save $50.
If you have any uncertainty about needing two inputs, even occasionally → Scarlett 2i2. The $50 is insurance worth buying.
If you record a podcast, music with another person, or any two-microphone setup → Scarlett 2i2, no debate.

Consider These Alternatives Too

At the 2i2's $169 price point, the Audient EVO 4 ($149) is worth considering — similar preamp quality with a unique Smartgain auto-level feature. At $219, the Scarlett 4i4 adds MIDI I/O and two additional line inputs for connecting hardware synthesizers and drum machines — the right upgrade path if you produce with hardware.

Best Free VST Plugins 2026: The Complete List by Category

The free plugin market has never been better. Some of the most widely used tools in professional studios cost nothing. This is the list — no filler, no obscure recommendations, no plugins that require a PhD to operate.

MP The Music Production Wiki Team Updated April 2026 14 min read Beginner

The idea that you need to spend hundreds of dollars on plugins before your music can sound professional is one of the most persistent and most damaging myths in music production. Some of the most capable mixing tools available — used on professional records, in professional studios, by professional engineers — are completely free. The plugins on this list are not consolation prizes for producers who can't afford paid tools. They are legitimate professional instruments that happen to cost nothing.

Free EQ Plugins

TDR Nova (Tokyo Dawn Records) is the finest free EQ plugin available and one of the best EQs at any price. Its four-band parametric design includes a dynamic EQ mode — where each band can be set to compress or expand rather than simply boost or cut — that adds capabilities typically found only in premium plugins costing $100 or more. Its frequency response is clean and transparent. Its interface is clear and immediately learnable. Professional engineers use TDR Nova alongside paid EQs not because they cannot afford alternatives but because it is genuinely excellent. Available for Windows and Mac, VST3 and AU.

Voxengo Marvel GEQ is a 16-band linear phase graphic EQ with a clean interface and accurate frequency response. Linear phase EQs introduce no phase shift — unlike minimum phase EQs which alter the phase relationship of frequencies around boost and cut points — making them ideal for mastering applications and situations where phase coherence is critical. At zero cost, Marvel GEQ gives producers access to a processing mode that most free EQs do not offer.

ReaEQ (Cockos) comes bundled with Reaper but is available as a standalone free plugin for any DAW. Its infinite-band parametric design, clean interface, and reliable performance make it a workhorse EQ that handles everything from surgical corrective work to broad tonal shaping. Its simplicity is a feature rather than a limitation — it does exactly what an EQ should do without unnecessary complexity.

Free Compressor Plugins

TDR Kotelnikov (Tokyo Dawn Records) is a mastering-grade compressor that costs nothing. Its program-dependent release behavior — where the release time adapts to the dynamics of the input signal rather than following a fixed setting — produces a natural, musical response that is difficult to achieve with fixed-release compressors. Its wideband and mid-side processing modes add mastering and mix bus capabilities that extend well beyond typical free plugin offerings. This is the compressor to use when you need transparent, high-quality dynamic control without spending money.

Molot GE (Vladislav Goncharov) is a Russian-designed compressor with a character reminiscent of Soviet-era hardware — thick, colored, with a distinctive harmonic saturation that adds density and weight to drums and mix buses. It is not a transparent compressor. It is a character compressor — one that adds something to the signal in addition to controlling dynamics. For producers who want the coloring of vintage hardware without the price of a hardware emulation plugin, Molot GE is an exceptional free tool.

Limiter No6 (Tokyo Dawn Records) is a free mastering limiter with five stages of processing — RMS compression, high-frequency limiting, peak limiting, clipping, and true peak limiting — that rivals paid mastering limiters costing $100–200. Its true peak limiting mode prevents inter-sample clipping during the digital-to-analog conversion process, making it suitable for streaming masters that need to meet platform true peak requirements. For independent producers mastering their own music, Limiter No6 provides professional-level ceiling control at no cost.

Free Reverb Plugins

Valhalla Supermassive is not just a good free reverb — it is one of the most creative reverb and delay plugins ever made, at any price. Developed by Valhalla DSP — the company behind the industry-standard Valhalla Room reverb — Supermassive specializes in enormous, otherworldly reverb and delay effects that range from subtle room enhancement to infinite, evolving soundscapes. Its WARP control modulates the delay feedback network in ways that create textures no conventional reverb can replicate. It has been used on professional records across every genre since its release. Download it immediately and use it forever.

OrilRiver is the finest free algorithmic reverb for conventional mixing applications — the natural room and hall sounds that Supermassive deliberately avoids. Its early reflection controls, diffusion settings, and frequency-dependent decay allow detailed shaping of the reverb character that most free reverbs do not offer. For producers who need convincing room, hall, plate, and chamber reverbs without paying for a premium reverb plugin, OrilRiver is the answer.

Dragonfly Reverb is an open-source reverb plugin with four algorithms — Room, Hall, Plate, and Vintage — that produce natural-sounding results across a range of reverb types. Its interface is simple and immediately usable. Its sound is clean without being sterile. For producers who want a reliable, free reverb that handles conventional mixing applications competently, Dragonfly is a dependable choice.

Free Synthesizer Plugins

Vital is the most significant free synthesizer release in years — a spectral warping wavetable synthesizer with capabilities that rival Serum, the paid industry standard it was clearly inspired by. Its three oscillators support wavetable, sample, and traditional waveform synthesis. Its modulation system — drag-and-drop modulation routing from any source to any destination — is powerful and intuitive. Its effects section includes a high-quality reverb, chorus, delay, distortion, and filter. A massive community of preset and tutorial creators has grown around Vital since its release. For producers who want a capable, professional synthesizer at zero cost, Vital is the answer.

Surge XT is an open-source hybrid synthesizer that combines wavetable, FM, and subtractive synthesis in a single instrument with a deep modulation system and an extensive factory preset library. Its sound engine is sophisticated enough for professional use — Surge XT has appeared on commercial releases across electronic music genres — while its open-source development model means it is continuously improved by a dedicated community. For producers who want synthesis depth beyond what Vital offers, Surge XT is the next step.

Dexed is a faithful emulation of the Yamaha DX7 — the FM synthesizer that defined the sound of 1980s pop music and remains one of the most sampled and referenced synthesizers in history. The DX7's electric piano, marimba, and bass sounds appear on thousands of records. Dexed loads original DX7 patch banks, of which thousands are freely available online, giving producers access to a library of FM synthesis sounds with genuine historical significance. For producers who want DX7 sounds without the cost of hardware or a paid emulation, Dexed is comprehensive and accurate.

Free Utility and Analysis Plugins

SPAN (Voxengo) is the most widely used free spectrum analyzer in music production. Its real-time frequency display, customizable display options, and accurate measurement make it an essential mixing reference tool — showing you what is happening in the frequency domain of any track or the full mix. Professional engineers use SPAN alongside paid analyzers because its accuracy and usability are genuinely excellent. Every producer should have it on their mix bus at all times.

Youlean Loudness Meter is the standard free loudness metering tool for producers mastering music for streaming platforms. It displays integrated LUFS, short-term LUFS, momentary LUFS, and true peak level — all the measurements needed to target platform normalization standards accurately. Its export function generates loudness reports that can be shared with collaborators or used to document mastering sessions. For producers who master their own music for streaming, Youlean is an essential and completely free tool.

MeldaProduction MFreeEffectsBundle is a collection of over thirty free plugins from one of the most respected plugin developers in the industry. The bundle includes EQs, compressors, reverbs, modulators, analyzers, and utilities — enough to build a complete mixing toolkit without spending a dollar. MeldaProduction's free plugins are not stripped-down versions of paid tools — they are fully functional professional processors that MeldaProduction releases free as a demonstration of their capabilities and a gift to the production community.

The Free Plugin Rule

Before you install any free plugin, read reviews from producers you respect and check that it is actively maintained — that the developer has released updates in the past year and that it is confirmed compatible with your current DAW version and operating system. The free plugin graveyard is full of excellent tools that were abandoned by their developers and no longer run on modern systems. Stick to plugins from developers with track records — Tokyo Dawn, Valhalla, Voxengo, MeldaProduction, and the open-source community — and every tool on this list will serve you reliably for years.

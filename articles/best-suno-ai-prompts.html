Best Suno AI Prompts: Complete Guide for Every Genre

MP
The Music Production Wiki Team
Updated May 2025 22 min read
Last verified: May 2025 — Suno's model updates regularly; prompt behaviour may shift with new versions.

Suno's output quality depends heavily on prompt quality. A vague prompt produces a generic result. A specific, well-structured prompt produces music that matches your intention. Understanding how Suno interprets prompts — what language it responds to, what level of specificity helps, and what tends to confuse or produce inconsistent results — dramatically improves your outputs without changing anything about the model itself.

What we'll cover: The anatomy of an effective Suno prompt, genre-specific proven prompt formulas, advanced techniques including Custom Mode and style tags, what to avoid, how to iterate effectively, and prompt examples for 15+ genres and moods.

The Anatomy of an Effective Suno Prompt

The most effective Suno prompts contain several components in a specific order that mirrors how music is described in professional contexts. Not every component is needed for every prompt — more specific prompts produce more targeted results, but sometimes a shorter prompt with the key details outperforms an overloaded long one. Here is the full framework:

Genre and subgenre: The most important element. "Hip-hop" is useful; "90s boom-bap hip-hop with dusty jazz samples" is much better. Be specific about the subgenre — Suno has learned from a vast corpus of music and understands genre distinctions at a nuanced level.

Tempo and energy: "Upbeat," "slow," "mid-tempo," "high-energy," "laid-back." These adjectives shape how Suno arranges the rhythm and overall feel. For more precision, you can specify BPM: "130 BPM trap" gives Suno a specific tempo target.

Instrumentation: List the instruments you want featured. "Acoustic guitar and piano," "808 bass, trap hi-hats, violin melody," "electric guitar, bass, live drums." Suno responds well to specific instrument references.

Mood and emotion: "Melancholy," "euphoric," "nostalgic," "tense," "triumphant," "romantic," "aggressive." Emotional descriptors shape the tonal and melodic choices Suno makes.

Production style or era: "80s production," "lo-fi," "overdriven guitars," "clean and polished," "analog warmth," "808-heavy." Production era and style references help Suno choose appropriate sounds and processing.

Vocal description: "Female vocalist," "male rapper," "harmonies," "no vocals — instrumental," "choir." If you're using Custom Mode with your own lyrics, the vocal style reference helps match the delivery to your text.

Theme or narrative: A brief thematic description helps Suno generate appropriate auto-lyrics if you're not providing your own: "summer romance," "late night driving," "overcoming hardship," "street life."

Pop Prompts

Pop works well in Suno because it's the most represented genre in any large music training dataset. The model has a sophisticated understanding of pop production conventions and delivers consistently strong results.

Contemporary pop ballad: "Emotional pop ballad, piano and strings, female vocalist with powerful belt, building to a cinematic chorus, lyrics about missing someone, production similar to Adele or Lewis Capaldi"

Upbeat summer pop: "Upbeat summer pop, 120 BPM, synth-driven with punchy drums, catchy chorus, female vocalist, feel-good lyrics about freedom and adventure, bright production"

Dark pop: "Dark pop, moody minor key, haunting female vocals with reverb, sparse verses building to dramatic chorus, themes of loneliness and isolation, production similar to Billie Eilish"

80s synth-pop: "80s synth-pop, Juno synthesizers, gated reverb drums, male vocalist, driving chorus, new wave influences, retro production"

Hip-Hop Prompts

Hip-hop is one of Suno's strongest categories. The model understands the distinction between different eras and subgenres with impressive accuracy.

Classic boom-bap: "90s East Coast boom-bap, sampled jazz piano loop, deep 808 baseline, boom-bap drums, relaxed male rapper with smooth delivery, introspective lyrics about city life"

Trap banger: "Hard trap beat, 140 BPM, heavy 808 bass slides, rapid hi-hat rolls, aggressive male rapper, boastful lyrics, dark minor key melody, stadium-ready production"

Melodic rap: "Melodic rap, emotional minor key piano, singing and rapping hybrid male vocalist, auto-tune on melodic hooks, lyrics about success and struggle, polished production"

Lo-fi hip-hop: "Lo-fi hip-hop instrumental, dusty drum samples with swing, jazz piano chords, vinyl crackle, mellow and nostalgic mood, no vocals, perfect for studying"

Electronic and EDM Prompts

Future bass: "Future bass, 150 BPM, wide lead synth, heavy sub-bass drop, build-up and release structure, vocal chops in the drop, euphoric and energetic mood"

Deep house: "Deep house, 122 BPM, warm bassline, jazzy chord progressions, subtle female vocal hook, late night atmosphere, Chicago house influences"

Drum and bass: "Liquid drum and bass, 174 BPM, rolling amen break, warm bass, atmospheric pads, soulful female vocal sample, flowing and energetic"

Techno: "Industrial techno, 135 BPM, driving kick drum, metallic percussion, hypnotic synth stabs, dark and relentless, no vocals, warehouse atmosphere"

Ambient electronic: "Ambient electronic, slow evolving textures, gentle reverb-drenched synthesizers, no percussion, peaceful and meditative, Boards of Canada influence"

Country Prompts

Modern country pop: "Modern country pop, acoustic guitar and banjo, female vocalist, upbeat chorus about small-town life, Nashville production, twangy but polished"

Classic country: "Classic country, Telecaster guitar, pedal steel, male baritone vocalist, honky-tonk piano, drinking song about heartbreak, 1970s outlaw country influence"

Country ballad: "Slow country ballad, acoustic guitar, slide guitar, emotional male vocalist, lyrics about loss and longing, sparse production that builds to an orchestral finale"

R&B and Soul Prompts

Neo-soul: "Neo-soul, Rhodes electric piano, warm bass, live drums with brushes, silky female vocalist, smooth harmonies, lyrics about a complicated relationship, D'Angelo influence"

Contemporary R&B: "Contemporary R&B, 808 bass, trap-influenced drums, falsetto male vocalist with autotune, sensual lyrics, minimal production with big emotional moments, Frank Ocean influence"

Classic soul: "Classic soul, Stax Records style, horns, organ, female vocalist with powerful voice, gospel-influenced, lyrics about devotion, 1960s production"

Jazz Prompts

Jazz is one of Suno's weaker genres — the model understands jazz instrumentation well but struggles with the improvisational quality and harmonic sophistication of actual jazz performance. Set expectations accordingly.

Smooth jazz: "Smooth jazz, saxophone lead, electric piano, walking bass, brushed drums, mellow and sophisticated, late night bar atmosphere"

Bossa nova: "Bossa nova, nylon string guitar, light percussion, breathy female vocalist, Portuguese and English lyrics, romantic Brazilian cafe atmosphere"

Modal jazz: "Modal jazz, Miles Davis influence, trumpet lead, piano comping, upright bass, cymbals, introspective and cool"

Ambient and Cinematic Prompts

Cinematic score: "Epic cinematic score, orchestral strings, French horns, timpani, building from quiet tension to triumphant climax, film trailer style, no vocals"

Dark ambient: "Dark ambient, drone textures, distant industrial sounds, eerie and unsettling, horror film atmosphere, no conventional melody or rhythm"

Meditation and focus: "Peaceful meditation music, Tibetan singing bowls, soft piano, gentle nature sounds, 432 Hz tuning, no percussion, perfect for meditation or sleep"

Advanced Prompting Techniques

Custom Mode for lyric control: Switch to Custom Mode to provide your own lyrics rather than Suno's auto-generated text. This is the most impactful way to improve the coherence and relevance of vocal content. Write lyrics with clear verse/chorus structure, indicate these sections with [Verse], [Chorus], [Bridge] markers, and Suno will follow the structure.

Style separation in Custom Mode: In Custom Mode, you can enter the style description separately from the lyrics. This allows you to be more detailed about the musical style without confusing Suno with too much information in a single field.

Reference artist prompting: Adding "in the style of [artist]" or "[artist] influence" to prompts often produces more genre-specific results. Suno has learned the stylistic characteristics of many artists and can approximate them. Use this for genre direction rather than expecting exact replication.

Iterating from a strong base: When a generation is close but not right, use the Extend or Continue feature rather than regenerating from scratch. This builds on what's working rather than starting over. Modify the prompt slightly — adding or removing one element — and regenerate the specific section that isn't working.

Generating instrumental versions: Add "instrumental, no vocals" or "no lyrics, pure instrumental" to any prompt. Suno follows this instruction reliably for most genres.

What to Avoid

Overloaded prompts with too many conflicting descriptors tend to produce unfocused results — Suno tries to satisfy all the requirements and satisfies none well. "Heavy metal jazz fusion with classical orchestra and hip-hop beats and country guitar" produces something confused rather than innovative.

Vague emotional descriptors without genre context — "a sad song" — produce generic results. Always pair emotional descriptors with genre context: "a sad indie folk song" or "melancholy trap music."

Very long prompts beyond about 50 words see diminishing returns. Suno's attention to the later parts of a very long prompt decreases. Front-load the most important information — genre, energy, key instrumentation — and keep the rest brief.

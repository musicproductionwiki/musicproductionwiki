Best Suno AI Prompts: The Complete Prompt Engineering Guide

First published May 2026 · Updated May 2026
MP
The Music Production Wiki Team
Updated May 2026 Beginner → Advanced
Accuracy Note

Suno's model updates regularly and prompt effectiveness changes between versions. These prompts were tested on Suno v4 in May 2026. Check suno.com/blog for current model updates that may affect behavior.

Quick Answer

Effective Suno prompts describe four things specifically: genre and subgenre, mood and energy level, instrumentation, and vocal style. Vague prompts produce generic results. Specific prompts like "mid-tempo indie pop, bittersweet reflective mood, fingerpicked acoustic guitar and piano, warm female vocals, 120 BPM, verse-chorus-verse structure" produce noticeably better output. This guide provides tested prompt templates for every major genre plus advanced techniques for controlling structure, lyrics, and style.

The Anatomy of an Effective Suno Prompt Beginner

Every high-quality Suno prompt contains these elements in roughly this order:

1. Genre + subgenre: Not just "hip-hop" but "lo-fi boom bap." Not just "electronic" but "melodic techno, Berlin-influenced." The more specific the genre reference, the more consistently Suno matches the target sound. Genre descriptors are the single highest-impact element in your prompt.

2. Mood and energy: "melancholic," "triumphant," "tense," "dreamy," "aggressive," "contemplative." Combine two mood descriptors for nuance: "dark but hopeful," "intense but cerebral," "nostalgic and warm." Suno responds well to emotional language.

3. Instrumentation: Name specific instruments. "Fingerpicked acoustic guitar" performs better than just "guitar." "Rhodes electric piano" is more specific than "keyboard." "808 sub bass" is more specific than "bass." Suno has a wide instrumental vocabulary — use it.

4. Vocal style: Male or female, register (baritone, tenor, alto, soprano), style (raspy, smooth, breathy, powerful), and production treatment (dry and close, reverb-heavy, vocoder-processed). For no vocals: "instrumental only" is reliable.

5. Optional precision elements: BPM ("120 BPM," "slow tempo," "mid-tempo"), song structure ("verse-chorus structure," "cinematic build," "four-to-the-floor"), era reference ("1970s soul," "early 2000s indie"), and production style ("lo-fi recording," "polished pop production," "raw live recording").

Tested Prompt Templates by Genre Beginner

Lo-fi Hip-Hop:
"lo-fi boom bap, nostalgic and melancholy mood, dusty vinyl crackle, mellow jazz piano samples, warm bass, slow hip-hop drums at 75 BPM, no vocals, chill study music aesthetic"

Pop:
"upbeat indie pop, hopeful and energetic, acoustic guitar, synth pads, driving drum kit, female vocals with light reverb, catchy chorus hook, 120 BPM, polished production"

Trap:
"dark trap, aggressive and menacing mood, 808 sub bass, hi-hat triplets, booming kick, atmospheric piano, male rapper vocals with autotune, 140 BPM, minimalist arrangement"

House:
"deep house, warm and hypnotic, four-on-the-floor kick, soulful vocal chops, Rhodes electric piano, sub bass, Chicago house influence, 125 BPM, late night club feel"

Ambient:
"ambient electronic, ethereal and expansive, long atmospheric pads, subtle field recordings, gentle arpeggiated synths, no percussion, drone bass, Brian Eno influence, meditative and slow"

R&B:
"contemporary R&B, sensual and emotional, lush vocal harmony, warm electric piano, 808 bass, minimal trap-influenced drums, breathy female lead vocals, smooth production, 90 BPM"

Rock:
"indie rock, energetic and raw, distorted electric guitars, driving bass guitar, live drum kit, male vocals, verse-chorus-bridge structure, slightly lo-fi recording aesthetic, 130 BPM"

"contemporary jazz, sophisticated and late-night, upright bass, brushed snare drums, jazz piano improvisation, muted trumpet, no vocals, intimate club recording, swing feel, 110 BPM"

Advanced Techniques Intermediate

Custom lyrics mode: Suno allows you to paste your own lyrics in the Custom mode. When providing lyrics, use brackets to indicate song sections: [Verse 1], [Chorus], [Bridge], [Outro]. Suno follows the structure you provide. Write lyrics with clear rhythmic phrasing — lyrics with natural speech rhythm set to music generate better vocal performances than lyrical structures Suno struggles to fit to a melody.

Style references: You can reference specific artists to indicate a production style, though results are variable. "Production style reminiscent of early Radiohead" or "vocal delivery similar to indie folk" gives Suno stylistic cues. Avoid using specific artist names if you're generating content for commercial use — style references work best as general direction rather than imitation.

Negative prompts: Some Suno versions support negative direction in prompts: "no electric guitar," "no drums," "avoid predictable chord progressions." These can help when Suno keeps defaulting to elements you don't want.

Regeneration strategy: Suno generates two variations per prompt. If neither is quite right, regenerate from the better variation rather than the original prompt — small variations with the same basic direction often get closer to the target than starting over. Keep notes on which prompt language consistently produces good results for your specific use case.

What Doesn't Work — Common Prompt Mistakes Beginner

❌ Vague Mood Language

"Good vibes" and "chill" are nearly meaningless to Suno. Be specific: "nostalgic and melancholy" performs better than "sad." "Triumphant and energetic" performs better than "happy." "Dark and atmospheric" performs better than "spooky." The more precisely you describe the emotional character, the more consistently the output matches your intent.

❌ Too Many Instructions

Prompts over 100 words often perform worse than focused 40-word prompts. Suno has a finite attention to prompt elements — too many competing instructions produce confused output that attempts to satisfy all of them partially rather than executing any of them well. Prioritize the most important elements and cut the rest.

❌ Requesting Complexity Suno Can't Deliver

"Complex time signatures," "quarter-tone microtonal tuning," and "atonal composition" are beyond Suno's reliable capability. Suno's strength is genre-conventional music that follows familiar patterns. The more your prompt requests departures from genre conventions, the less reliably Suno delivers. Work with the model's strengths for the best results.
